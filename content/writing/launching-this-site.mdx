The cheapest way to ship a portfolio is to grab a template, paste in three screenshots, and call it done. The most honest way is to treat the site as a craft project of its own.

I picked the harder path for one reason: a portfolio of work and a portfolio of craft are different things, and senior engineers should ship both.

## What "craft" means here

When I say I want every interaction to feel _crafted_, I mean specific things:

- **Springy on enter, easy on exit.** Things arriving use a bezier-spring curve. Things leaving use a smooth ease-out. Linear curves are reserved for opacity only.
- **No animation longer than 600ms** outside the lab page. Longer is annoying on the second visit.
- **Reduced-motion is honored everywhere.** Not just `motion: none` — a graceful fade-only fallback for each animation.

<Callout type="note">
  Lab pieces are allowed to break the 600ms rule. They're meant to be re-watched.
</Callout>
